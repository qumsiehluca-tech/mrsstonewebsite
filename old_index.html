A Separate Service

MCA Debt Restructuring

If your business took on one advance, then another to cover the first, and the daily withdrawals are now squeezing your operating account — you are not alone, and you are not out of options.

A merchant cash advance isn't structured like a loan. There's no set monthly payment and no fixed payoff date you can simply plan around. Instead, the funder takes a percentage of your future receivables — often pulling directly from your bank account every business day — secured by a lien on those future receivables.

When that's working, it's manageable. When you're carrying several at once, the combined daily draws can take a real bite out of the money you need to make payroll and keep the lights on. That's the situation restructuring is built for.

Here's the honest part: Archangel is not a debt-settlement mill, and we don't make promises we can't keep. What April offers is a genuine legal opinion on where you stand — your agreements, your liens, your exposure — and then a practical path forward.

  • An attorney's read of your actual agreements. Not a sales pitch — a lawyer telling you what your contracts really say and what leverage you have.
  • Restructuring through cooperative, vetted lenders. Much of the work happens hand-in-hand with funders we've personally vetted — checked specifically to confirm their collection practices are fully within the law.
  • Protection of your rights at every step. Aggressive or unlawful collection tactics are more common than they should be. Knowing the line — and having counsel who'll hold it — changes the conversation.
  • A plan you can actually operate under. The goal is a structure your daily cash flow can survive, so the business stays open.

Common Questions

Worth knowing before you apply.

Is a merchant cash advance the same as a loan?

No, and the difference matters. A loan has a fixed payment and a set payoff date. A merchant cash advance is a purchase of your future receivables — repayment is a percentage of your sales, usually drawn directly from your business bank account, and it's secured by a lien on those future receivables. There's no fixed monthly bill. We make sure you understand exactly how that works for your account before anything is signed.

What does "attorney-led review" actually get me?

Before you sign, April reviews the funding agreement and gives you a plain-English legal opinion on it — what the lien covers, how the daily or weekly draw is calculated, and where your rights stand. Most business owners are never offered a lawyer's read of these documents. With Archangel, it's simply part of the process.

I already have several advances. Can you help?

Yes — that's what the MCA Debt Restructuring service is for. April reviews your existing agreements and gives you a legal opinion on your position, then, where it fits, works with vetted cooperative lenders to restructure the debt into a structure your cash flow can actually sustain. A review is a starting conversation, not a commitment.

What do you mean by "vetted lenders"?

Archangel only works with funders it has personally vetted. A large part of that vetting is confirming their collection practices are fully within the law. If a funder is known for aggressive or unlawful collection conduct, they're not in our network. That protects you, and it's not something most brokers bother to do.

What are the basic requirements to apply?

Generally we look for a business operating for at least six months, steady monthly revenue, and an active business bank account. Credit is considered but is rarely a disqualifier on its own. The quickest way to know where you stand is to start an application — it puts you under no obligation.
